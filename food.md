Food For Thought Fest — Full Dossier
Short description (one-liner)

Food For Thought Fest (FFTF) is an annual South-Asia–focused gastronomy festival organized by the South Asian Association for Gastronomy (SAAG). It combines a food fair (Food Fest), a ideas program (Thought Fest) and entertainment (Fun Fest) to celebrate regional culinary heritage, contemporary gastronomy and food policy.

Core facts (quick reference)

Organizer: South Asian Association for Gastronomy (SAAG) — project run by the founders of Haute Services (Maneesh & Sonali Baheti).

First edition / Founded: 2015 (inaugural edition at India Habitat Centre, New Delhi).

Format: Multi-day festival with three core zones — Food Fest, Thought Fest, Fun Fest (demos, tastings, panel discussions, cultural performances).

Typical attendees: chefs, hospitality professionals, policy makers, students, food writers, embassies/diplomats and the public.

Mission & positioning

SAAG and FFTF aim to be the authoritative South-Asia gastronomy platform: a networking hub for food professionals, a knowledge consolidator for culinary traditions, and a thought leadership forum where food culture, policy and innovation meet. The festival stresses “regional unity through culinary diversity.”

Festival structure & recurring features

Food Fest: curated food court and pop-ups representing South Asian countries and notable restaurants (specialty pavilions such as Luxury Hotel Court, Taste of Luxury etc. in some editions).

Thought Fest: panels, keynote talks and interviews with chefs, writers, policy-makers and cultural figures discussing food heritage, sustainability, food & policy, and gastronomy as culture.

Fun Fest: evening entertainment, cultural performances and family-friendly experiences.

Editions & notable locations (selected timeline)

2015 (1st) — India Habitat Centre, New Delhi. Inaugural festival; featured culinary representations from SAARC nations and a Thought Fest program with panels and guests.

2016–2019 — The festival ran in Delhi-area venues (Taj Palace, malls, and Aerocity). 2019 edition was held at GMR Aerocity (GMR Square), New Delhi (Dec 14–15, 2019).

2022 — Festival returned to Delhi-area programming (press & site show 2022 highlights).

2024 (and onward) — FFTF launched in Ahmedabad at the Sabarmati Riverfront Event Centre (organised with Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation in recent editions). Site shows FFTF 2024 and subsequent Ahmedabad editions; FFTF announced continuing editions into 2025.

Note: FFTF is described on the official site as an annual event since 2015; some years (e.g., the COVID period) saw gaps or scaled-back activity — check the festival “Past Fests / Highlights” pages for year-by-year details.
